The first four weeks with a founder are less about writing code and more about telling the truth.

## Scoping is the first deliverable

Before anything ships, we scope hard: what the idea actually needs, what it doesn't, and where the real risk lives. We say what we think — not what's comfortable to hear. A good scope saves months.

## Architecture you can defend

From there we set an architecture that fits the stage of the company, not the stage of our ambitions. Then we build, hands-on, as a partner — from napkin sketch to a shipped, defensible product.
