Tax software usually assumes you already think like an accountant. For most people, that assumption is where the product fails.

## Start from the confusion, not the form

Building Salli meant starting from the questions real people actually ask — not the boxes on a government form. We mapped the local filing scenarios first, then designed a guided flow over a rules engine that could explain *why* each number was what it was.

## AI where it reduces confusion

We added AI assistance only where it lowered the cognitive load — clarifying a term, catching a likely mistake — never where it added novelty for its own sake. Legibility was the whole product goal, and legibility is a design constraint, not a feature.
